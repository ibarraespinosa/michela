# About Me

I'm Dr. Michela Sanguedolce, a mechanical engineer from the hills of Calabria, now working at the University of Maryland's James Clark School of Engineering. My work lives at the intersection of **advanced manufacturing, biomaterials, and medicine** — I design and build the tiny structures that help the human body heal itself.

I grew up in Rende, a small town nestled between the Sila mountains and the Tyrrhenian Sea. The forests of Calabria taught me to see patterns in complexity. The sea taught me patience. I bring both to my work.

---

### What Drives Me

I believe the best engineering doesn't just solve problems — it disappears into the body so seamlessly that the patient forgets it was ever there. That's the bar I hold myself to.
